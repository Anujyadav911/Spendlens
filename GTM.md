# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## 🎯 Target User
The exact target user is the **Founding Engineer** or **Operations Manager** at a **Series A startup (20–60 people)**. 
- **The Pain:** They just raised money, their AI tool spend (Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT) has ballooned from $200/mo to $3,000/mo, and the CFO is asking questions.
- **The Behavior:** They are currently googling "Cursor vs Copilot enterprise pricing" or "How to save on OpenAI API costs."

## 🔍 Search & Scroll Behavior
They hang out on:
- **X (Twitter):** Following accounts like @levelsio, @swyx, or @karpathy.
- **Subreddits:** r/SaaS, r/LocalLLaMA (looking for cheaper alternatives), and r/startups.
- **Slack/Discord:** "Lounge" or "Engineering" channels in YC, South Park Commons, or local startup communities.

## 🚀 The $0 Budget Plan (First 100 Users in 30 Days)
1.  **The "Compare Your Stack" Thread (Day 1-5):** Post a data-rich thread on X comparing the "Hidden Overspend" of the top 3 AI tools. Use screenshots of the SpendLens audit as proof. Tag the tool founders (Cursor/Anthropic).
2.  **Launch on Hacker News (Day 7):** Position it as a "Public Service Tool" for the community. Title: "Show HN: We built a tool to see if you're overpaying for AI."
3.  **Cold DM "AI Heavy" Founders:** Identify companies on LinkedIn/X that just announced "AI-first" pivots. Send a personalized Loom: "Hey, saw the pivot! We built this free audit tool because we found most 20-person teams save $500/mo on day one."
4.  **The "SaaS Audit" Giveaway:** Partner with a small startup-focused newsletter (e.g., "The Rundown AI") for a featured "Tool of the Week."

## 📈 The Unfair Distribution Channel
The unfair advantage is **Credex's own secondary market inventory**. We don't just tell you that you're overpaying; we are the *only* ones who can offer you a 30% discount on the very tools you're using. We can embed the "Buy through Credex" button directly into the "Switch" recommendation.
